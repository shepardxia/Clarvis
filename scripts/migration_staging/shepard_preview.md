## 31. [2026-02-14] (from academic)

Japanese Noise Tradition (Japanoise): Merzbow, founded by Masami Akita in 1979, named after Kurt Schwitters' Merzbau. Most prolific Japanoise artist. Kansai region (Osaka/Kyoto) was the crucible — Kansai no wave emerged late 1970s/early 1980s blending punk, art-school experimentalism, industrial, performance art. Hijokaidan, Hanatarash (Yamatsuka Eye).

Boredoms formed Osaka 1986. Members: Yamatsuka Eye (vocals), Yoshimi P-We (drums). Early noise-punk, Pop Tatari (1992) brought international attention. Toured with Sonic Youth (1992) and Nirvana (1993). Evolved through Super Roots series toward ecstatic psychedelia. Vision Creation Newsun (1999): tribal drumming, cosmic synths, ritualistic transcendence.

Boris formed Tokyo 1992, named after a Melvins track. Bridge Japanese noise with Western sludge, drone, stoner metal. Genre range: experimental rock, noise rock, drone metal, shoegaze, dream pop, J-pop, crust punk. Boris-Merzbow collaboration began late 1990s, ~8 releases. Boris framed noise as "Japanese blues."

Melt-Banana formed 1992. Noise-grind with rhythmic precision and pop instincts. Became duo 2013: vocalist Yako, guitarist Agata.

Les Rallizes Denudes formed 1967 at Doshisha University, Kyoto. Core member Takashi Mizutani. Psychedelic drone, feedback guitar, proto-shoegaze. Released almost nothing officially — existed through bootlegs. Bassist Moriaki Wakabayashi orchestrated the 1970 Yodogo Incident (Red Army JAL hijacking). Influential on Boris, Sunn O))), others.

## 32. [2026-02-14] (from academic)

Japanese Post-Hardcore and Screamo: envy formed Tokyo 1992. Fused American post-hardcore (Fugazi, Husker Du, Big Black) with post-rock dynamics. A Dead Sinking Story (2003) created template for post-rock-influenced screamo worldwide. Frontman Tetsuya Fukagawa's "sandpaper screams." Departed 2016, returned 2018. The Fallen Crimson (2020).

Heaven in Her Arms emerged Tokyo mid-2000s, named after a Converge Jane Doe track. Third wave Japanese screamo — darker, more metallic than envy. Both envy and Heaven in Her Arms influenced Deafheaven (San Francisco), whose Sunbather (2013) brought blackgaze to global attention.

Asunojokei formed 2014 Taito ward Tokyo. Absorbed Deafheaven's blackgaze back into Japanese context, blending post-black metal with Dir en Grey's extremity and Shimokita-kei riffs. Toured Europe 2023 supporting Boris. Feedback loop: Japanese screamo → American blackgaze → new Japanese post-black metal.

Ling Tosite Sigure formed Saitama 2002. Mathematically precise post-hardcore drawing from shoegaze and screamo. Bridges math rock and hardcore. TK (male vocals) and 345 (female vocals). Extended Number Girl's complexity into progressive territory.
